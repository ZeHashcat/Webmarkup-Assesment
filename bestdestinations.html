Antarctica

See it while it’s still there! Global warming is a thing, and that thing causes the massive changes for the wildlife and climate of the Antarctic. Aside from that, the peace and solitude in combination with the natural beauty make the Antarctic a must see location. A trip to the Antarctic is very expensive though, costing roughly 6000-9000 euro’s. If you’ve got the money however it offers an unforgetable experience.

Tokyo

Do you even need us to state reasons for going to Japan? It’s also an unforgettable experience, offering many sights in the form of urban architecture and more ancient sights such temples and such. Not to mention the eccentric products and wonderful food Japan has to offer. It’s also one of the largest cities in the world with approximately 14 million people. The cost of a return trip starts around 750 euro.

Moscow

One of the largest cities to exist and home to the famous Kremlin, is a location you must have been to at least once in your life, besides, what could be better then snow and vodka. Ancient as it is, Moscow used to be home to many a great duke as well as the tsars, nowadays it’s still the seat of the government. A trip to Moscow starts around 175 euro.

Rome

Once a great empire, it went in to decline shortly after the implantation of the year system we use to this very day. The city bearing this name still contains many vestiges of the once great empire though, and that makes it a must see area for history buffs. To this day it still bears S.P.Q.R or Senātus Populusque Rōmānus proudly on it’s coat of arms, reminiscent of the times the majority of Europe was under the control of an empire. Prices for tickets start as low as 105 euro.

New York

Once under dutch control and called New Amsterdam, New York is the hallmark of the eastern United States of America, much like it’s western counterpart Los Angeles. Wall Street once used to sit along the outer wall of New York, hence the name Wall Street. When people think of a metropolis New York is often the first one that comes to mind, so if you love urban areas this is definitely one you want to visit. Prices for a ticket start at 620 euro.

Alexandria

Founded by the great Alexander the Great in 331 BC, Alexandria quickly replaced the ancient Memphis as the capital of Egypt before Cairo became the capital nearly a millennium later. Although the actual Library of Alexandria burned down in antiquity you can still visit Bibliotheca Alexandrina today! Other important sites include Montaza Palace, the statue of Ptolemy II Philadelphus and the Planetarium Science Center. Prices for a ticket start at 325 euro.
